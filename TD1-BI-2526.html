TD1- Business Intelligence
Educational objective: The objective of this tutorial is to discuss with the students the concepts already
acquired relating to companies, the information system and decision support, to then make the link and
introduce the concept of business intelligence.
1. Reminder Concepts:
a. The company (definition, main objectives, role of the management function - planning, CTRL,
organization, problem solving,...)
b. b. Reminder of the importance of information systems for organizations, particularly businesses,
in operational, technical, and strategic management and in decision support.
c. Reminder of the types of decisions, and discussion of the information that must be provided for
decision-making in the three levels of management.
d. The Information System → is the IS that will provide the company with information, it will help
the company in its management.
2. Introduction of BI and the components of a BI system in an interactive approach:
a. « Business intelligence » (BI) refers to a broad range of procedures, approaches, technologies,
and equipment used to extract useful information from unprocessed data and enable organizations
to make well informed decisions. In order to improve business performance and competitiveness.
b. In the Gartner IT Glossary, « BI is an umbrella term that includes the applications, infrastructure
and tools, and best practices that enable access to and analysis of information to improve and
optimize decisions and performance »
c. BI system : It is a decision support information system that enables managers to analyse and
interpret complex data about the company and its environment using simple tools..

• The key components of Business Intelligence include
1
:
o Data sources : Gathering data from disparate sources, both internal (e.g., transactional
systems, CRM databases, ERP databases) and external (e.g., market research, social media),
o Data integration : raw data are gathered (extracted), transformed and integrated into a
centralied repository for analysis.
o Data Warehousing and Storage: Storing structured and unstructured data in a centralized
data warehouse, optimizing data accessibility and scalability for analytics.
o Analytics and Reporting: Employing various analytical techniques, such as descriptive,
diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics, to derive insights and generate actionable
reports and dashboards ; interactive data visualization tools are used to convey complex
insights in a meaningful and accessible manner, facilitating data driven decision-making at all
levels of the organization.
• Significance of Business Intelligence System : the growing significance of Business Intelligence
lies in its ability to empower organizations with timely, accurate, and actionable insights, enabling
them to:
o Enhance Decision-Making:
o Improve Operational Efficiency: BI helps identifying process inefficiencies and
bottlenecks, BI helps streamline operations, enhance productivity, and reduce costs.
o Gain Competitive Advantage: BI enables organizations to identify market trends, customer
preferences, and emerging opportunities, fostering innovation and market responsiveness.
o Enhance Customer Experience: By analysing customer behaviour and preferences, BI
facilitates personalized marketing strategies, customer segmentation, and targeted
engagement.
